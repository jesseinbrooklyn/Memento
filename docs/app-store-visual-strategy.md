# Memento — App Store Visual Strategy

A complete playbook for designing the App Store listing screenshots, icon, and promotional assets for Memento V1.

---

## Competitive Landscape

Before designing, here's what Memento is up against and where the gap lives.

### Direct Competitors (Stoic Philosophy)

**Stoic. (Journal & Mental Health)** — The biggest player. 4M+ users, Apple "App of the Day" featured. Their screenshots lean heavily on bright gradient backgrounds (purples, blues, greens) with white device frames. Every screenshot has a bold caption like "Beat Stress in 120 Seconds" or "Guided Journals." The tone is wellness-first, philosophy-second. Lots of feature-list energy. They look like a mental health app that happens to reference Stoicism.

**Stoa (Daily Stoic Philosophy)** — More philosophically grounded. Their screenshots use softer earth tones, lifestyle photography, and serif typography. Captions like "Stoicism, Meditation, Therapy." More contemplative feel, but still within the standard wellness-app visual language: clean white cards, colorful accents, rounded corners.

**Stoic Meditations** — Minimal presence. Basic screenshots, no device framing, no captions. Not a serious visual competitor.

### Adjacent Competitors (Wellness/Journaling)

**Headspace** — The gold standard for app store presentation. Bright illustrations, character-driven, playful. Their screenshots tell a story: "Be kind to your mind" → guided meditations → sleep → focus. Every frame is a brand moment, not a feature demo.

**Calm** — Dark, nature-themed. Uses full-bleed landscape photography (mountains, water, forests) behind device mockups. Serene and premium. Closer to Memento's mood than any competitor.

**Day One** — Clean, minimal, photography-focused. Their screenshots emphasize the journal interface itself: rich text, photos, maps, timeline views. "Your journal for life." Trust-building through polish.

**Balance** — Dark backgrounds, glowing accents, personalized feel. Their screenshots emphasize AI-driven personalization with captions like "Meditation, personalized."

### The Gap Memento Fills

Every competitor either looks like a pastel wellness app (Stoic., Headspace, Reflectly) or a nature-calm meditation app (Calm, Balance). Nobody looks like ancient philosophy feels: dark, weighty, mortality-aware, built in marble and gold. Memento's visual identity IS its differentiator on the App Store. Lean into the classical darkness. Don't soften it. Don't add gradients. The difference is the strategy.

## Screenshot Strategy

### Format Requirements

Per Apple's guidelines and the shipping guide:

| Display | Resolution | Minimum | Recommended |
|---------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| 6.7" (iPhone 15 Pro Max) | 1290 x 2796px | 3 | 6 |
| 6.1" (iPhone 15 Pro) | 1179 x 2556px | 3 | 6 |

Google Play: 1080 x 1920px minimum, up to 8 screenshots.

### Design Framework

Based on the 2026 best-practice research, here's the approach tailored to Memento:

**Layout:** Each screenshot = dark background + device mockup + caption text + subtle ornamental element.

**Background:** Solid dark (#14100a to #0e0b07 gradient, matching the app's own background). No photography, no nature shots, no patterns. The darkness IS the brand. Optional: very faint classical texture (parchment noise at 3-5% opacity) to add warmth without competing.

**Device Frame:** Thin, dark iPhone frame (titanium black, matching Pro hardware). The app screen glows within it like candlelight in a dark room. The frame grounds the screenshot and helps users imagine holding the app.

**Typography:** Cinzel for captions (matching the app). Gold (#c4a35a) on dark. Letter-spaced. All caps for headlines. Cormorant Garamond italic for supporting lines.

**Ornamental Elements:** Hairline gold rules, small classical motifs (laurel fragments, thin borders). Very restrained. These aren't decorations — they're the visual language of ancient inscription.

**Flow:** The 6 screenshots follow a narrative arc: Philosophy → Practice → Depth → Privacy.

## The 6 Screenshots (Sequence)

### Screenshot 1: THE HOOK
**Caption:** REMEMBER YOU WILL DIE
**Subcaption:** *Let that shape how you live.*
**Screen shown:** Awaken screen — skull, time, "12,564 days remaining"
**Purpose:** Stop the scroll. This is the most important frame. The mortality counter is unlike anything else in the store. Lead with the thing no other app dares to show.
**Design note:** Centered device, generous dark space above and below for the text to breathe. The skull and gold glow from the app should be the visual anchor.

### Screenshot 2: THE MORNING PRACTICE
**Caption:** FACE EACH DAY WITH INTENTION
**Subcaption:** *A single focus. Not a to-do list.*
**Screen shown:** The morning flow — daily Stoic quote + intention chips visible
**Purpose:** Show what the daily ritual looks like. This is the habit the user is buying into. The quote from Marcus Aurelius or Seneca gives immediate intellectual credibility.
**Design note:** Slightly angled device or offset left, with the caption on the right. Let the quote text be partially readable — it sells the depth.

### Screenshot 3: THE WISDOM
**Caption:** 100 VOICES ACROSS 2,000 YEARS
**Subcaption:** *Marcus Aurelius. Seneca. Epictetus. Viktor Frankl.*
**Screen shown:** Sapientia screen — a quote with author attribution, source reference, and historical context paragraph visible
**Purpose:** Establish the intellectual weight. The source citations (e.g., "Meditations 2.11") signal that this isn't a generic quotes app — it's curated scholarship.
**Design note:** Device centered. The quote text should be legible enough to intrigue but the caption does the selling.

### Screenshot 4: THE RECKONING
**Caption:** YOUR LIFE IN DAYS
**Subcaption:** *Grounded in actuarial data. Not wishful thinking.*
**Screen shown:** Tempus screen — the large "12,564" days remaining with the ornate hourglass and "54.75% OF LIFE ELAPSED" bar
**Purpose:** The life calculator is a unique feature. The hourglass illustration is visually stunning. This screenshot sells the "this app is different" feeling.
**Design note:** Let the hourglass art dominate. Pull the device slightly larger in frame to showcase the illustration quality.

### Screenshot 5: THE PRACTICE
**Caption:** DISCIPLINE WITHOUT STREAKS
**Subcaption:** *No points. No confetti. Just the work.*
**Screen shown:** Virtus screen — practice calendar dot grid showing a month of practice dots, morning/evening settings
**Purpose:** Address the anti-gamification philosophy directly. The dot calendar is visually clean and philosophically honest. This screenshot differentiates from every "maintain your streak!" competitor.
**Design note:** This is a quieter screenshot. Let the simplicity of the dot grid speak. The caption does the heavy lifting on positioning.

### Screenshot 6: THE PROMISE
**Caption:** NO ACCOUNTS. NO CLOUD. YOURS ALONE.
**Subcaption:** *Everything stays on your device.*
**Screen shown:** Scriptum journal screen — showing a journal entry (or empty state with the contemplative prompt)
**Purpose:** Close with trust and privacy. In a world of data harvesting, "everything stays on your device" is a powerful closing argument. The journal screen shows the most personal feature — and the promise that it's private.
**Design note:** This is the "seal the deal" frame. Consider a slightly pulled-back view with a subtle gold border framing the entire composition, like a closing page of a book.

## Icon Design Direction

The app icon needs to work at every size (1024x1024 down to 60x60) and read instantly in a sea of pastel wellness icons.

**Concept:** A classical vanitas skull rendered in gold line art on a near-black background (#14100a). Minimal detail — the skull should read at 29x29px. Consider a simplified frontal view with prominent eye sockets and a subtle laurel wreath or hourglass element.

**What to avoid:** Gradients, 3D renders, text in the icon, too much detail, any color besides gold and black. Don't make it look like a pirate app or a Halloween icon — the line weight and artistry must signal "philosophy" not "horror."

**Reference mood:** Think: engraved bookplate, Roman coin, museum catalog cover.

### Name
**Memento**

### Subtitle (30 char max)
`Stoic daily practice` (20 chars)

Alternatives: `Remember. Reflect. Return.` (27 chars) / `Mortality. Wisdom. Practice.` (29 chars)

### Description
> Remember that you will die. Let that shape how you live.
>
> Memento is a daily Stoic practice built on the ancient tradition of memento mori. Each morning, face your remaining days and set a single intention. Each evening, reflect on what went well, where you fell short, and what didn't matter as much as you thought.
>
> 100 quotes from Marcus Aurelius, Seneca, Epictetus, Musonius Rufus, Montaigne, and Viktor Frankl. A personal commonplace book for the wisdom that strikes you. A life calculator grounded in actuarial data. A journal for the words you need to write but no one else needs to read.
>
> No accounts. No cloud. No streaks. No confetti. Everything stays on your device.
>
> Dark, quiet, and honest — like the philosophy it carries.

`stoic,memento mori,meditation,journal,philosophy,marcus aurelius,seneca,daily practice,mortality,wisdom`

### Option A: Canva (MCP Available — Not Connected)
Canva has an MCP connector in the registry. If you connect it, I can search templates, create designs, and export directly. Canva has App Store screenshot templates with device mockups already built in. This is the fastest path to professional results — you'd pick a dark template, I'd help customize it with Memento's assets, and we'd export at the exact required dimensions.

**Pros:** Fast, template-driven, no design skill needed, direct MCP integration.
**Cons:** Templates may fight Memento's unique aesthetic. May need heavy customization to get the classical feel right.

### Option B: Figma (MCP Available — Not Connected)
Figma has an MCP connector too. More design control than Canva. Better for pixel-perfect work and maintaining exact brand tokens. There are free App Store screenshot templates on Figma Community.

**Pros:** Full design control, exact brand-token matching, professional output.
**Cons:** Steeper learning curve, more manual work.

### Option C: Computer Use (Your Desktop)
I can drive design apps directly on your Mac. If you have Figma, Canva, or even Keynote/Pages installed, I can operate them through screen control. Keynote is actually excellent for app store screenshots — precise positioning, easy export at exact pixel dimensions, and it's already on your Mac.

**Pros:** Uses tools already on your machine, I do the clicking.
**Cons:** Slower iteration, depends on what's installed.

### Option D: Code-Generated (HTML/React → Screenshot)
I can generate the screenshot compositions as HTML or React components with exact pixel dimensions, using your actual design tokens from the codebase. You'd screenshot them from a browser or use a headless browser to export PNGs.

**Pros:** Exact brand match (uses your actual color/font tokens), reproducible, version-controllable.
**Cons:** Requires some setup, less intuitive to iterate on.

**Start with Option A (Canva via MCP)** for speed, with **Option D (code-generated)** as backup if Canva's templates can't nail the classical dark aesthetic.
